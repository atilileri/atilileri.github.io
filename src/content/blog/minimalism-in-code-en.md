Software is often bloated. Features creep in, dependencies grow out of control, and architecture resembles spaghetti. But what if we applied the principles of Swiss-modernist graphic design to the way we write code?

Less abstraction, clearer intent, and a strong foundational grid (or in our case, design patterns).
